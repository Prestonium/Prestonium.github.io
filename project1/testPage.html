An Ethical Framework for Evaluating Political Structures

This application provides an interactive guide to a universal ethical framework for evaluating political systems. It is grounded in two core principles: **Universal Equality** and **Collective Self-Determination**. The goal is not to prescribe solutions, but to offer a consistent lens for analyzing justice in our world.

The Two Pillars of Justice

Universal Equality

Rights are bestowed by personhood, not group membership. A state's legitimacy depends on its ability to provide equal protection and political agency to all individuals.

Collective Self-Determination

All peoples have a right to organize politically to preserve their culture, security, and identity. This right, however, cannot justify violating universal equality.

Challenges, Limitations, and the Moral Imperative

While robust, this framework is not without its challenges. Its idealism can be a point of critique, as a truly free and fair democratic process is difficult to achieve in conflict zones. Furthermore, the language of "self-determination" or "equality" can be co-opted by bad actors to justify oppression. The framework's success depends on the intellectual honesty of those applying it.

Despite these limitations, the framework‚Äôs moral imperative remains. It is a powerful tool for revealing the inherent injustices of political systems that privilege some over others. It reveals that the most significant ethical failures of modern political systems are not merely the absence of a specific solution, but the failure to uphold the fundamental rights of every person and every people.

The framework‚Äôs core claim is that political legitimacy is not a matter of historical right or military might, but of moral performance. It demands that all states and all peoples engage in the difficult work of building societies that are just for all.
